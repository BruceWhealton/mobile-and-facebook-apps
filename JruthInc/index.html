Welcome to JRuth, Inc.

This is the home page for JRuth Inc., a 501(c)3 Non-Profit Charitable Organization located in Durham, NC. JRuth, Inc. was founded by Johnetta Ruth Alston. In 2012, JRuth, Inc. celebrated 10 years of success. Ms. Alston provides housing, education/training and other services to the needy, homeless, transient, and ex-offender population.

Home Mission

Mission Statement: JRuth, Inc.

The mission of JRuth, Inc. is to strengthen individuals and families in need to become more productive, educated, and motivated citizens of their community. The organization offers several programs that teach a variety of life skills and assist people in meeting their basic needs. Ultimately, JRuth seeks to guide participants on the road to self-sufficiency.

Johnetta Ruth Alston - Founder, CEO of Jruth, Inc.

Johnetta Ruth Allen Alston, a 48 year-old mother and grandmother is a native of Durham, North Carolina. In founding, organizing, and maintaining several community service programs throughout Durham, Raleigh, Chapel Hill and Butner, Ms. Alston has made it her life’s work to advocate for those in the community who cannot advocate for themselves including homeless, transient, ex-offender, veteran, and low income men and women. She has received education at Rutledge College (Business/Accounting), North Carolina Central University (Business/Accounting), and Durham Technical Community College (Business). With this formal training, as well as, over 1700 instructional hours and practical community work experience in both the non-profit (Few Gardens Community Center, Secretary) and private sectors (Central Carolina Bank, Head Vault Teller; GTE/Verizon—Management/Associate). Ms. Alston gained the skills necessary to fulfill her vision of helping individuals and families in need become productive, educated, and most importantly, motivated to work and live in their community. Out of this vision, JRuth, Inc. was founded in 2002.

Ms. Alston serves as the Executive Director of JRuth, Inc., a non-profit 501(C)3 organization that assists those in need through four programs: 1. Self Sufficiency (the educational component); 2. Rental Housing Placement (JRuth Manor); 3. Employment; and 4. Home Ownership. In addition to these programs, JRuth Manor was established in November 2007, opening its doors to individuals and families in transition whose goal is to promote the culture of family.

In conjunction with JRuth, Inc., Ms. Alston is a training coach and instructor for Durham Technical Community College, where she successfully teaches Life Skills, Financial Literacy, and Employability Skills within Durham, Butner and Orange Counties as well as in Federal Correctional Facilities. As a constituent to Good Work, Inc., Ms. Alston takes great pride in facilitating the Building Your Business entrepreneurial training classes in Durham and Chapel Hill. She has constructed two curriculums: 1) Directions By Reflections which is the employ-ability skills training, that consist of seven lessons. 2) Grants Workshop 101- complete instructions and tools for successful grant writing.

As a member of the NAACP-Durham, Ms. Alston serves on the executive committee as Housing Chairperson. Most recently, she accepted the challenge in 2007 to host the Durham Summer Camp for Women, an extension of Building Together Ministry’s decade old camp in Raleigh. Durham Summer Camp for Women is now in its second season and provides its participants with fellowship, a weekly dinner, and childcare for the night, at low or no cost. Camp is also available to the Hispanic population.

Ms. Alston’s endeavors are made possible by her grant certification through HUD, and she is the recipient of several local and state awards, including the City of Durham’s Community Development Block Grant (CDBG), the City of Raleigh’s Community Service Grant, IBM and Commerce Bank—New York. Further helping the programs are partnerships with local organizations, include the Food Bank of North Carolina, Good Work, World Relief, CAARE, End Poverty Network, TROY House, Community Success Initiative, ReBuild Durham, Durham Economic Resource Center, RESTORE-Criminal Justice Resource Center and many others.

Through JRuth, Inc. and the other programs she is involved with, Ms. Alston has made it her mission to serve those in need strive for self-sufficiency and make her community a better and more productive place to live.

JRuth Programs include:

Housing Placement

The Housing Placement Program allows individuals and small families to receive safe and affordable shelter at minimal cost. JRuth works in partnership with several property owners and provides a housing director/manager who helps participants reach and maintain their housing and personal goals. For those who wish to own a home, JRuth can work with you to develop a plan. To date, two families have received the Non-Traditional Mortgage Home-Ownership Grant.

JRuth Manor

JRuth Manor is a transitional home where individuals receive support services and experience "life as a family." JRuth Manor is supervised by our Housing Director/Manager-- Subrina Byas. The disciplined structure of family life at JRuth Manor offers residents the guidance and support they need to become self-sufficient members of this community. JRuth Manor collaborates with several local organizations to provide a safe haven for those in need. We are a furnished facility. Our housing committee is dedicated to assisting you in providing family and self-sufficiency.

Employment

JRuth's Employment program works with local businesses to provide participants with job referrals and training opportunities. JRuth offers classes on employ-ability, interviewing, and in building skills in resume writing to assist people in their job search. JRuth does not discriminate against any willing applicant, and thus has developed "offender friendly" partnerships, matching qualified individuals, who need a second chance, with employers who are willing to give it.

Self-Sufficiency

JRuth's SelfSufficiency program consists of educational classes with curriculum designed for personal growth and development. Current classes include: Directions By Reflection; FDIC Money Smart Financial Literacy Series; Balanced Living & Smart Saving; Grant Writing 101; Employ-ability Skills; Assertiveness Training and Personal Growth and Development; Entrepreneurship & Small Business Training. Some of these classes are offered in partnership other local institutions, such as Durham Technical Community College and Good Work, Inc.

Direction By Reflection is a curriculum based upon personal reflection of previous life events and gaining skills to enhance your future. The curriculum is an eight week program based around YOU, the individual, and your desire to seek personal guidance and coaching within your life. Each week, individuals will read, observe and discuss themes and ideas in an effort to achieve personal growth and development.

Overview: Lesson I - Who Am I? Exploring what it means to live a life of INTEGRITY.

Lesson 2 - Chinese Bamboo Learning discipline and perseverance by studying the life cycle of a plant.

Lesson 3 - Two Wolves Experiencing moral introspection through Native American storytelling.

Lesson 4 - 23rd Psalm Examining guidance and direction in the life of a shepard.

Lesson 5 - There is a Hole in the sidewalk
